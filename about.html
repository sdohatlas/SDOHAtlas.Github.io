About

The relationship of social and neighborhood characteristics with health outcomes is established but remains poorly understood because of complex, multidimensional factors that vary across space. We develop a multidimensional SDOH typology of the continental US at tract level for 2014 using exploratory factor analysis and regionalization algorithms, and estimate neighborhood-level mortality rates using SDOH indices in the City of Chicago, controlling for violent crime. Four identified SDOH components of advantage, isolation, opportunity, and accessibility explain over 75.6% of the variance for all census tracts in the continental United States for the time period, and cluster as 7 multidimensional neighborhood SDOH typologies. There is a strong association between SDOH indices and age-adjusted mortality in Chicago. During a time where there is increasing attention paid to SDOH, this analysis provides actionable intelligence for key stakeholders with respect to focus of interventions.
